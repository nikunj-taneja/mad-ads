# Desire drivers — Life-Force 8 lens

Use this file to document the human desires the brand can credibly connect to. It is a strategy lens, not a demographic label and not a claim that every customer shares the same motive.

## The Life-Force 8

The eight durable desire families commonly used in direct-response strategy are:

1. survival, enjoyment of life, and life extension;
2. enjoyment of food and drink;
3. freedom from fear, pain, and danger;
4. sexual companionship;
5. comfortable living conditions;
6. superiority, winning, and keeping up;
7. care and protection of loved ones;
8. social approval.

Learned wants—such as beauty and style, cleanliness, efficiency, quality, convenience, bargains, curiosity, and education—can support an argument, but should connect to a deeper payoff when evidence permits.

## Means-end chain

Every concept should connect a product truth to a human payoff:

```text
[physical attribute or verified fact] → [functional benefit] → [downstream human payoff]
```

Neutral examples of the reasoning shape:

- compact form → easier to carry every day → less friction and more confidence while away from home;
- repairable construction → longer useful life → pride in buying deliberately rather than repeatedly replacing;
- guided setup → fewer mistakes and faster first success → feeling capable and in control.

These examples illustrate structure only. They are not approved claims for this brand.

## Rules

- Choose one primary driver per concept.
- Treat the driver as a hypothesis, not a customer fact.
- Cite the evidence that makes the chain plausible.
- If a feature cannot reach a credible payoff, reconsider whether it deserves to lead.
