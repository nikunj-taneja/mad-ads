# Hook structure library

Hooks must be filled with verified specifics and matched to awareness, sophistication, persona, and angle. Template numbers make later performance analysis possible.

## Contrarian and reversal

1. “The popular advice about [problem] is making [specific outcome] worse.”
2. “We removed [expected feature]. Here is what improved.”
3. “The reason I would not recommend this to [non-fit buyer].”

## Curiosity gap

4. “I tested [specific number/category]. One detail changed the result.”
5. “Watch what happens when [concrete action or demonstration].”
6. “There is one line in [receipt/review/specification] most people miss.”

## Pain and problem

7. “If [specific frustrating situation] keeps happening, check [mechanism].”
8. “The [time/place] when [current solution] stops working.”
9. “Before you buy another [category], test this.”

## Authority and experience

10. “After [verified experience], this is the mistake I notice most.”
11. “A [verified credential] explains why [unexpected result].”
12. “What [verified group/source] taught us about [problem].”

## Specific result and demonstration

13. “[Verified number] changed after we changed one thing.”
14. “[Concrete before] versus [concrete after], under the same conditions.”
15. “One [unit/action] at [time] and the result at [later time].”

## Mistake-based

16. “Do not make this [category] decision until you check [specific].”
17. “The expensive mistake hidden inside [familiar behavior].”
18. “I thought [belief]. The evidence showed [correction].”

## Story and transformation

19. “At [specific moment], [unexpected event] happened.”
20. “We built this after [verified origin incident].”
21. “[Person/source ID] almost gave up on [goal] until [turn].”

## High-retention short

22. “[Number] seconds. One visible difference.”
23. “Look at [specific visual detail].”
24. “This is [familiar thing], except [meaningful difference].”
